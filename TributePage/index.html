Some Facts about Nikola Tesla

  1. Tesla was born in 1856 in present-day Smiljan, Croatia. At the time, Croatia was part of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.
  2. Tesla contracted cholera when he was 17 years old and was on bed rest for nine months. Tesla’s first job was as a drafter at an engineering firm in Slovenia.
    3. Tesla spoke eight languages: Serbian, English, Czech, German, French, Hungarian, Italian, and Latin.
  3. In addition to studying both math and physics at the Technical University of Graz, Tesla also studied philosophy at the University of Prague. Tesla claims the idea of alternating currents came to him in a vision.
  4. Tesla immigrated to the United States in 1884 when he was 28 years old.
  5. Tesla arrived in New York in 1884 and started working as an engineer for Thomas Edison. After Edison refused to pay Tesla for his work, Tesla abruptly quit. This caused a lifelong feud between the two innovators.
  6. When Tesla couldn’t find work after quitting his job at the Edison Company, he had to dig ditches for $2 per day.
  7. Tesla and the American author Mark Twain were friends. In 1894, Tesla took a photo of Twain in one of the first pictures ever lit by phosphorescent light.
  8. In 1895, Tesla’s lab in New York burned down, destroying years’ worth of notes, equipment, and models.
  9. In 1901, Tesla partnered with J.P. Morgan and gave up 51% interest in his patents and inventions in exchange for funding.
  10. Tesla claimed he invented a death ray called the Teleforce.
  11. In 1915, word spread that both Tesla and Edison were going to be honored with the Nobel Prize for physics. However, neither man received the prize. It was rumored that Tesla refused to share it with Edison.
